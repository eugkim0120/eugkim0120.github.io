## Current research: AI surrogate modelling for inertial confinement fusion

### Neural operator models for inertial confinement fusion

In my ongoing Visiting Fellow research at Oxford with Prof. Peter Norreys, I am developing **AI surrogate models for inertial confinement fusion**. The goal is to approximate expensive PDE-based physics simulations with compute-efficient neural operator models, making it possible to explore high-dimensional fusion design spaces much faster than repeatedly running full first-principles simulations.

This research sits at the intersection of scientific machine learning, fusion physics, and optimisation. I am particularly interested in models that can learn mappings between physical inputs and simulated plasma / fusion outputs, while remaining accurate, stable, and useful inside downstream parameter sweeps and optimisation loops.

The broader aim is to make fusion simulation workflows more interactive: faster surrogate models can support sensitivity analysis, experimental design, Bayesian optimisation, and rapid screening of candidate configurations before committing expensive compute to high-fidelity simulations.

## Earlier fusion simulation work

### Energy gain of wetted-foam implosions with auxiliary heating for inertial fusion studies

R W Paddock, T S Li, **E Kim**, J J Lee, H Martin, R T Ruskov, S Hughes, S J Rose, C D Murphy, R H H Scott, R Bingham, W Garbett, V V Elisseev, B M Haines, A B Zylstra, E M Campbell, C A Thomas, T Goffrey, T D Arber, R Aboushelbaya, M W Von der Leyen, R H W Wang, A A James, I Ouatu, R Timmis, S Howard, E Atonga, P A Norreys

*Plasma Physics and Controlled Fusion*, Vol. 66, 025005 (2024)

[**View on IOP Science**](https://iopscience.iop.org/article/10.1088/1361-6587/ad15ee) · DOI: [10.1088/1361-6587/ad15ee](https://doi.org/10.1088/1361-6587/ad15ee) · Open Access (CC BY 4.0)

This study introduces an analytic model to predict the performance of inertial fusion implosions combining two promising techniques: low-convergence-ratio wetted-foam layers for improved hydrodynamic stability, and auxiliary electron-beam heating for hotspot ignition. Bayesian optimisation was used to efficiently explore the high-dimensional PDE-based simulation parameter space, identifying regions of interest for experimental design.
